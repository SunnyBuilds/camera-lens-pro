Modern camping technology enhances safety, convenience, and comfort. This guide helps you choose the right electronic gear and accessories for your outdoor needs.

## Headlamps & Lighting

- Lumen output - 200-300 lumens for general camp use, 400+ for hiking
- Battery life - Look for 10+ hours on medium settings
- Red light mode - Preserves night vision and doesn't disturb others
- Rechargeable vs disposable - USB rechargeable more convenient, disposable as backup
- Water resistance - IPX4 minimum for rain protection
- Beam distance - 50-100 meters for most camping activities

## Portable Power Solutions

- Power banks - 10,000-20,000 mAh capacity for multi-day trips
- Solar panels - Backup charging for extended wilderness trips
- Battery capacity - Higher mAh means more device charges
- USB ports - Multiple ports charge several devices simultaneously
- Durability - Waterproof and shockproof ratings protect investment
- Weight considerations - Balance capacity with portability

## GPS & Navigation Devices

- Screen size - Larger screens easier to read but heavier
- Battery life - Full-day minimum, week-long for extended trips
- Map capability - Preloaded topographic maps essential
- Satellite features - Two-way messaging and SOS for safety
- Smartphone integration - Sync routes and waypoints
- Mounting options - Handlebar or pack strap mounts

## Camp Communication

- Two-way radios - Essential for group coordination in areas without cell service
- Range considerations - 2-5 miles realistic in wilderness terrain
- Weather alerts - NOAA weather radio feature warns of storms
- Emergency whistle - Simple backup signaling device
- Satellite communicators - Garmin inReach or SPOT for true wilderness

## Photography & Documentation

- Camera protection - Weatherproof cases or bags protect electronics
- Extra batteries - Cold weather drains batteries faster
- Memory cards - Bring multiple high-capacity cards
- Portable chargers - Keep devices powered throughout trip
- Lens cleaning kit - Essential for maintaining image quality
- Tripod or stabilizer - Capture better landscape and night shots

## Comfort & Convenience Electronics

- Portable speakers - Waterproof Bluetooth for camp entertainment
- E-readers - Lightweight entertainment for downtime
- Weather stations - Monitor conditions for activity planning
- Portable fans - Battery-powered cooling for hot nights
- Heated gear - Battery-powered socks or gloves for cold camping
- Action cameras - Document adventures hands-free

## Power Management

- Power strips - Limited outlets at campgrounds require splitters
- Extension cords - Reach distant power sources
- Surge protectors - Protect devices from power fluctuations
- Battery organizer - Keep spare batteries sorted and accessible
- Charging station - Centralized area for device charging
- Solar lights - Ambient lighting without battery drain
